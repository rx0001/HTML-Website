Essential Experiences

Leisure Centre and Beach

Although Kirkcaldy may be a small town, it offers lots of activities such as hiking, sports and leisure activities. The Kirkcaldy leisure centre allows you to use facilities such as the gym, swimming, and sports equipment for sports such as Badminton, and basketball. Located very closely to the centre is the beach which has a designated walking path and places to rest on a hot summer day. The shopping centre in and outside towns attracts many fashionable shops and on street parking allows easy access to the town and use of the shopping centre.

Kirkaldy Castles

The town is home to not one but two historical parks with Beveridge Park and ravens craig park the latter even including its own still functioning castle. Many marathons and 5km runs have been conducted on these parks making it a hotspot for tourists and homeowners alike. Football fans will love Starks Park home to Raith Rovers FC an title winning club, which has had highly skilled individuals playing under its banner. A weekend game and a head down to the local restaurant sets the town as a great place to spend the weekend, as it has many buildings to accommodate needs.

Lochore Meadows Country Park

The Kirkcaldy area is home to the beautiful Lochore Meadows Country Park, with the heart of the park being the loch which provides opportunities for swimming bird watching and kayaking alike. The Park is also a great place for walking, picnicking, and barbecuing, birdwatching, cycling. Golf, trout fishing, horse riding, mountain biking, water sports and orienteering are available. The beach and adventure playground are favourites with the kids including the spiders web climbing frame. There is also a cafe for that all important food stop.

    Famous Castles of Kirkcaldy

      • Ancient Castles

      The existence of Ravenscraig Castle was due to the request put in my Queen Mary of Gueldres in 1460. Over time famalies changed until it eventually came into the possession of the Sinclair Family, who transformed the Castle into the fortress it is today. In the 21st Century the castle is now, no longer used for watching ships by the sea, but now as a site that visitors can go to learn about the rich history that lies behind it.
